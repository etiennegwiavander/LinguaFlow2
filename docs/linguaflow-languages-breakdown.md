# LinguaFlow Languages & Lesson Categories - Complete Breakdown

## Overview
LinguaFlow currently supports **English language learning** across 6 specialized categories with multiple proficiency levels following the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR).

---

## üìö Available Lesson Categories

### 1. **Grammar** 
**Levels:** A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2 (All 6 levels)

**Description:** Comprehensive grammar instruction covering fundamental to advanced English grammar concepts.

**Level Breakdown:**
- **A1 (Beginner):** Basic sentence structure, present simple, articles
- **A2 (Elementary):** Past tenses, comparatives, basic modals
- **B1 (Intermediate):** Present perfect, conditionals, passive voice
- **B2 (Upper-Intermediate):** Advanced tenses, reported speech, complex sentences
- **C1 (Advanced):** Subjunctive mood, inversion, advanced clause structures
- **C2 (Proficiency):** Nuanced grammar, stylistic variations, formal/informal registers

**Content Types:**
- Grammar explanations with examples
- Interactive exercises
- Fill-in-the-blank activities
- Sentence completion quizzes

---

### 2. **Conversation**
**Levels:** A1, A2, B1, B2, C1 (5 levels)

**Description:** Practical conversational English for real-world communication scenarios.

**Level Breakdown:**
- **A1 (Beginner):** Greetings, introductions, basic questions
- **A2 (Elementary):** Shopping, ordering food, simple conversations
- **B1 (Intermediate):** Expressing opinions, discussing hobbies, travel conversations
- **B2 (Upper-Intermediate):** Debates, complex discussions, idiomatic expressions
- **C1 (Advanced):** Nuanced discussions, cultural topics, abstract concepts

**Content Types:**
- Dialogue examples
- Role-play scenarios
- Discussion questions
- Vocabulary in context
- Pronunciation practice

---

### 3. **Business English**
**Levels:** B1, B2, C1 (3 levels - Intermediate to Advanced)

**Description:** Professional English for workplace communication and business contexts.

**Level Breakdown:**
- **B1 (Intermediate):** Email writing, phone calls, basic presentations
- **B2 (Upper-Intermediate):** Meetings, negotiations, business correspondence
- **C1 (Advanced):** Executive communication, complex negotiations, business strategy discussions

**Content Types:**
- Business vocabulary
- Professional email templates
- Meeting scenarios
- Presentation skills
- Negotiation language
- Industry-specific terminology

---

### 4. **English for Travel**
**Levels:** A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2 (All 6 levels)

**Description:** Practical English for travelers and tourism situations.

**Level Breakdown:**
- **A1 (Beginner):** Airport basics, hotel check-in, asking for directions
- **A2 (Elementary):** Booking accommodations, ordering at restaurants, shopping
- **B1 (Intermediate):** Handling travel problems, cultural exchanges, tour interactions
- **B2 (Upper-Intermediate):** Complex travel situations, local interactions, travel planning
- **C1 (Advanced):** Cultural immersion, detailed travel discussions, travel writing
- **C2 (Proficiency):** Travel journalism, cultural analysis, advanced travel scenarios

**Content Types:**
- Travel vocabulary
- Situational dialogues
- Cultural tips
- Practical phrases
- Emergency situations

---

### 5. **English for Kids**
**Levels:** A1, A2, B1, B2 (4 levels - Beginner to Upper-Intermediate)

**Description:** Age-appropriate English lessons designed specifically for young learners.

**Level Breakdown:**
- **A1 (Beginner):** Colors, numbers, animals, basic greetings
- **A2 (Elementary):** Family, school, hobbies, simple stories
- **B1 (Intermediate):** Describing experiences, storytelling, expressing preferences
- **B2 (Upper-Intermediate):** Complex narratives, opinions, creative writing

**Content Types:**
- Interactive games
- Story-based learning
- Visual vocabulary
- Fun exercises
- Age-appropriate dialogues
- Engaging activities

**Special Features:**
- Child-friendly interface
- Gamified learning
- Visual learning aids
- Progress tracking for parents

---

### 6. **Pronunciation**
**Levels:** A2, B1, B2 (3 levels - Elementary to Upper-Intermediate)

**Description:** Focused pronunciation training for clear and accurate English speech.

**Level Breakdown:**
- **A2 (Elementary):** Basic sounds, word stress, simple intonation
- **B1 (Intermediate):** Connected speech, sentence stress, common pronunciation patterns
- **B2 (Upper-Intermediate):** Advanced intonation, accent reduction, fluency techniques

**Content Types:**
- Phonetic exercises
- Minimal pairs practice
- Stress and intonation patterns
- Audio examples (3 per lesson)
- Pronunciation drills
- Common pronunciation mistakes

## üéØ CEFR Level Descriptions

### A1 - Beginner
- Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions
- Can introduce themselves and others
- Can ask and answer simple questions

### A2 - Elementary
- Can understand sentences and frequently used expressions
- Can communicate in simple routine tasks
- Can describe aspects of their background

### B1 - Intermediate
- Can understand main points of clear standard input
- Can deal with most situations while traveling
- Can produce simple connected text

### B2 - Upper-Intermediate
- Can understand complex texts on concrete and abstract topics
- Can interact with fluency and spontaneity
- Can produce clear, detailed text

### C1 - Advanced
- Can understand demanding, longer texts
- Can express ideas fluently and spontaneously
- Can use language flexibly for social, academic, and professional purposes

### C2 - Proficiency
- Can understand virtually everything heard or read
- Can express themselves spontaneously and precisely
- Can differentiate finer shades of meaning
